# 3. Non-Claims And Trust Boundaries

This work is intentionally conservative.

It does not claim audited circuit security, complete global data coverage,
production key management, or superiority over all validation providers.

Trust boundaries:

- The issuer must be trusted for the credential it issues.
- The source data must be trusted for the region or quality claim.
- The verifier policy must declare accepted issuers, roots, scopes, and freshness.
- The proof system must be audited before production.
- Raw address material must remain outside public signals and public logs.
